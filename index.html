Applied Digital
In Practice · Colo vs. hyperscale vs. neocloud

Colo (Equinix, Digital Realty) leases space/power and sells interconnection; hyperscalers (AWS/Azure/Google) build their own halls to run their own cloud; neoclouds (CoreWeave) are new GPU-only clouds that often rent capacity from others and resell it as AI compute. Knowing which model a player uses tells you how they compete — on network density, on scale, or on GPU availability.

TechRepublic
In Practice · PUE, quickly

Power Usage Effectiveness = total facility power ÷ IT power. A PUE of 1.5 means 50% overhead (cooling, losses) on top of the compute; 1.1 is elite. Liquid cooling wins partly because it slashes the cooling share of that overhead — so when a vendor touts water/energy savings, translate it to "lower PUE" to compare apples to apples.

TechRepublic
In Practice · Reading demand in megawatts

The industry now measures AI capacity in MW of IT load, not square feet. Rough intuition: 1MW powers on the order of ~500–1,000 modern GPUs once you include cooling and overhead — so a "250MW lease" is a genuinely huge compute cluster. When you see deal sizes in MW, mentally convert to "how many GPUs and how much grid" to gauge scale.

LLM Stats – AI News
In Practice · What "SWE-Bench" measures

SWE-Bench (and the harder SWE-Bench Pro) tests whether a model can resolve real GitHub issues in real codebases — not trivia. A score like 80.3% means it fixed ~4 of 5 tasks. It's a better signal than generic "IQ" benchmarks for coding usefulness, so when labs quote it, they're claiming practical software-engineering ability.

Network World
In Practice · Spine-leaf (Clos) fabric

AI clusters ditched the old three-tier network for a flat spine-leaf design: every "leaf" (rack) switch connects to every "spine" switch, so any server is a predictable two hops from any other — critical when thousands of GPUs must exchange gradients in lockstep. It's why AI backends need lossless, high-radix 800G Ethernet: one slow or congested link stalls the whole training job.

CNCF
In Practice · The shared responsibility model

Gartner projects 99% of cloud security failures through 2026 are the customer's fault — almost always misconfiguration. The cloud provider secures the infrastructure ("security of the cloud"); you secure what you put in it — IAM, network rules, data ("security in the cloud"). Most breaches aren't clever hacks; they're an exposed storage bucket or an over-permissive role.

PMI Agile 2026
In Practice · Risk vs. Issue

A risk might happen (future, uncertain — manage with a mitigation/response plan); an issue is happening now (certain — manage with a corrective action and an owner). Sloppy PMs call everything a "risk," hiding problems that need action today. In status updates, split them: "Top risks (watching)" vs. "Open issues (acting)" — it signals control and maps straight to the PMP's Process and People domains.
