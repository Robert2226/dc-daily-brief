01Equinix
In Practice · Cross-connects are the actual product, not the building

It's easy to think of a colo provider like Equinix as a real-estate company that happens to hold servers, but the durable moat is the interconnection layer: physical or virtual cross-connects between one customer's cage and another's (a bank to its market-data feed, an enterprise to an AWS Direct Connect port) inside the same facility. Power and space are commodities any developer can build; a dense, already-populated ecosystem of the other networks and clouds you need to reach isn't — that's why Equinix's "who else is already in this building" pitch matters more to sticky revenue than raw megawatts. When you're evaluating a facility (xScale or retail colo), the interconnection density and named ecosystem partners are the harder-to-replicate asset, not the shell.

Morningstar
In Practice · A "1GW campus" headline is a grid interconnection, not a delivered data center

When Lancium or QTS announce a gigawatt figure at groundbreaking, that number describes the transmission capacity secured for the site's full build-out over years — Phase I typically energizes at a small fraction of that, the same pattern last week's Helios campus showed (133MW delivered against a 526MW eventual target). Two unrelated 1GW Texas announcements landing in the same week is itself a signal of how commoditized the "gigawatt campus" press release has become — read the phased MW figures, not the headline number, to judge what's actually buildable near-term.

Generation adequacy reports are due July 20 from all six regional grid operators (PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE, NYISO), the first checkpoint under FERC's June 18 show-cause orders requiring each to justify or reform how data centers and other large loads interconnect — with tariff revisions due within 60 days of the original order.

FERC

HumansFirst, co-founded by former Tea Party organizer Amy Kremer, coordinated 142 protests across 42 states on July 18 — Texas (18 events), Georgia (11), and California (8) led — citing water use, grid cost pass-through, and lack of community input; polling cited in coverage puts only 14% of Americans willing to support a data center in their own community.

Daily Signal
In Practice · Section 206 is FERC's tool for forcing a rule change, not just reviewing one

Most FERC action approves or rejects tariffs utilities propose. A Section 206 "show cause" order flips that: FERC asserts an existing rule may no longer be just and reasonable and puts the burden on the grid operator to either defend it or rewrite it on FERC's clock. Ordering all six RTOs/ISOs simultaneously, rather than acting case-by-case as complaints arise, signals FERC treats large-load interconnection as a systemic problem, not a one-off dispute — worth knowing the difference between "FERC reviewed a filing" and "FERC forced one" when you're reading grid-policy coverage.

Futurum Group
In Practice · A backlog a company says it can't fulfill is a different signal than one it just hasn't billed yet

Most backlog disclosures (Digital Realty's, CoreWeave's) describe signed contracts still working through construction — a timing problem. Microsoft explicitly attaching "power constraints" to $80B of unfulfillable Azure orders is a capacity-ceiling problem: no amount of faster construction execution fixes it if there's no interconnection available to energize the building. When you see a backlog number, the follow-up question is which kind it is — a construction queue that clears with time, or a hard external constraint that doesn't.

Mistral AI
In Practice · A quality-gated delay is a different failure mode than a compute-gated one

When a lab slips a launch because it's still waiting on GPU capacity or fine-tuning compute, that's a resourcing problem with a knowable end date. Google scrapping and rebuilding Gemini 3.5 Pro's base model over hallucination rates means the eval suite caught something the team judged unshippable — a governance decision, not a scheduling one. The tell is which lever a lab pulls: more compute buys you a later date on the same model; a rebuild means the thing that shipped internally didn't clear the bar at all.

06Networking

Nvidia pushes Spectrum-X into head-to-head competition with Cisco and Arista's core Ethernet business

Network World
In Practice · Scale-up vs. scale-out is the distinction that explains why Nvidia is suddenly a switch vendor

"Scale-up" networking connects GPUs within a server or rack to act as one giant accelerator (NVLink/NVSwitch — Nvidia has always owned this layer). "Scale-out" networking connects racks and pods to each other across the data center floor — traditionally Ethernet or InfiniBand, and traditionally Cisco/Arista/Juniper territory. Spectrum-X is Nvidia extending from the layer it already controls into the layer it didn't, on the argument that AI traffic patterns (many-to-many, latency-sensitive) need the scale-out fabric co-designed with the GPU, not bought separately. That's the real stake in "Nvidia vs. Cisco" headlines — it's a bet about where the design boundary between compute and network should sit.

Sophos: phishing and malicious email overtook unpatched vulnerabilities as the top ransomware root cause

Sophos' State of Ransomware 2026 report (2,158 orgs surveyed across 17 countries) found malicious email (26%) and phishing (24%) now outrank vulnerability exploitation (18%, down from 32%) as ransomware entry points — and that MFA was present in 97% of credential-based attacks that succeeded anyway.

Dark Reading
In Practice · MFA present isn't the same as MFA that resists phishing

Push-notification and SMS/OTP-based MFA still fundamentally trusts the user to make a correct judgment call under pressure — "approve this login" push-bombing and real-time phishing proxies (adversary-in-the-middle kits) both exploit exactly that trust gap, which is why Sophos found MFA deployed in 97% of successful credential attacks. Phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware keys or passkeys) removes the human judgment call entirely by cryptographically binding the credential to the legitimate site's domain, so a look-alike login page simply can't complete the handshake. If your org counts "MFA enabled" as a solved risk without asking which kind, this report is the data point that assumption is wrong.

08Program & PM

PMI Agile 2026 sets its program: human-centricity as the counterweight to AI-driven complexity

The July 26–28 conference's theme, "MORE, Together — Shaping the Future with Agility," centers on a keynote from Dr. Johan Roos arguing that human-centricity is the load-bearing value of the Manifesto for Enterprise Agility precisely because AI raises the stakes on judgment, curiosity, and collaboration rather than removing the need for them.

Agile Alliance
In Practice · "Human-centricity" as a design principle, not a soft-skills add-on

It's tempting to read AI-and-agility conference themes as reassurance messaging aimed at anxious practitioners, but the sharper version of the argument is structural: as AI agents absorb more of the mechanical coordination work (status chasing, schedule updates — the same shift driving PMI's ECO reweighting toward Business Environment), the judgment calls that remain for humans get harder, not easier, because they're the ones AI explicitly couldn't resolve. Practically, that means a team's investment in facilitation, stakeholder judgment, and cross-functional communication skills should scale up as automation increases, not down — the easy coordination work disappearing doesn't shrink the human skill requirement, it concentrates it.
