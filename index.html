In Practice · REITs and the dividend requirement

Equinix just logged its 11th consecutive annual dividend increase. As a REIT, it's legally required to distribute at least 90% of taxable income to shareholders — which is why capacity expansion gets funded through debt and equity issuance rather than retained earnings. Understanding this financing structure explains why land/power acquisition timelines and capital raises are always in the background of Equinix's growth story.

In Practice · Neocloud vs. hyperscaler vs. colo

A colo provider (Equinix) rents space/power/interconnection and lets tenants bring their own gear. A hyperscaler (AWS/Azure/GCP) owns everything end-to-end and sells managed cloud services. A "neocloud" (CoreWeave, Lambda) sits in between — owns GPU fleets, leases the building/power, and sells raw AI compute. Meta reselling excess capacity blurs the hyperscaler/neocloud line and is exactly the kind of competitive dynamic worth tracking as capacity math shifts.

In Practice · Why cooling dominates the power bill

During the current U.S. heatwave, cooling alone is consuming up to 40% of a data center's electricity — and that share climbs as outside temps rise, since chillers work harder against a higher delta-T. This is the practical reason liquid/direct-to-chip cooling is displacing air-cooled CRAC units for high-density racks: moving heat via liquid is dramatically more efficient than moving it via air, which directly lowers PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness — total facility power ÷ IT equipment power).

In Practice · Power is the new capacity planning bottleneck

For years, GPU supply was the constraint on AI buildouts; now it's grid interconnection queues and substation lead times (often 3-5+ years). When you see a hyperscaler announce a "10 GW" deal, that's the real ceiling on how much compute they can ever turn on at that site — translate MW commitments into rough GPU-cluster scale (a 100MW site supports roughly tens of thousands of high-end GPUs) to gauge how serious an announcement actually is.

In Practice · Reading a model release like a spec sheet, not a press release

Look past the headline for three numbers: context window (how much text/code it can hold in one pass), the benchmark suite used (SWE-bench for coding, MMLU for general knowledge — and whether it's the harder "verified" variant), and price per million tokens. A model that's "smarter" but costs 3x more per token isn't necessarily the better engineering choice for a given workload — that's the same cost/benefit tradeoff you'll make on a project business case.

In Practice · Why AI clusters need different fabric than a normal data center

Traditional east-west traffic in an enterprise DC is bursty and forgiving of some latency/packet loss. GPU training traffic (all-to-all collective operations across thousands of GPUs) needs a lossless, low-latency, non-blocking spine-leaf (Clos) fabric — a single dropped packet stalls the whole training job. That's why RoCE (RDMA over Converged Ethernet) and ultra-high port-density switches like the N9100 exist: they're purpose-built for GPU-to-GPU traffic patterns, not general enterprise networking.

In Practice · Why leaked cloud keys are worse than leaked passwords

An Azure PAT (Personal Access Token) or storage access key isn't just a login — it's a scoped credential that can be embedded in CI/CD pipelines, scripts, and config files, often with far broader permissions than a human account and no MFA prompt to stop misuse. This is the real argument for short-lived tokens and secrets managers (Azure Key Vault, HashiCorp Vault) over long-lived static keys checked into repos: it shrinks the blast radius when — not if — a repo gets exposed.

In Practice · Risk vs. Issue — say the right word

A risk is an uncertain future event that may or may not happen (the NY moratorium becoming permanent, a vendor missing a milestone). An issue is something that has already happened and needs resolution now (a permit denial that already landed). Conflating the two in a status report is a fast way to lose stakeholder trust — risks belong in a risk register with probability/impact and a response plan; issues belong on an action log with an owner and a due date. The July ECO leans hard on this distinction under Process domain tasks.
